Service 02

Sales Tax Audit Appeals

When a CDTFA audit produces an assessment you believe is incorrect, excessive, or based on flawed methodology, you have the right to appeal. The appeals process is formal and procedurally demanding — experienced representation significantly improves outcomes.

We represent clients at every level of the California sales tax appeals process, from informal appeals conferences through formal proceedings before the Office of Tax Appeals (OTA).

Levels of appeal we handle

  • CDTFA Appeals Bureau — informal appeal conferences
  • Office of Tax Appeals (OTA) — formal hearing representation
  • Petition for rehearing and reconsideration at OTA
  • Judicial review in California Superior Court when warranted

How we build your appeal

A strong appeal is built on facts, records, and legal authority — not just disagreement with the auditor's conclusions. We analyze the audit workpapers in detail, identify errors in sampling methodology, incorrect application of law, and factual mischaracterizations. We draft the formal appeal brief, gather supporting documentation, and present your case persuasively.

Common grounds for appeal

  • Flawed statistical sampling that overstates the error rate
  • Incorrect taxability determinations on specific transactions
  • Failure to account for valid exemption certificates
  • Improper use-tax assessments on exempt purchases
  • Misapplication of California sales and use tax law
  • Procedural errors that affected the audit outcome

Time limits on appeals are strict. Contact us immediately to protect your right to appeal a CDTFA determination.

Service 04

Refunds & Overpayments

California businesses frequently overpay sales tax — collecting and remitting tax on transactions that are actually exempt, or paying use tax on purchases that don't require it. These overpayments often go unclaimed simply because businesses don't know to look for them.

We conduct systematic refund analyses across your transaction history to identify overpayments and file refund claims with the CDTFA to recover money you're owed. In many cases, refund recoveries significantly exceed consulting fees.

Common sources of overpayment

  • Sales to exempt purchasers without proper documentation at time of audit
  • Sales of exempt products or services incorrectly taxed
  • Manufacturing and research equipment exemptions not claimed
  • Resale transactions incorrectly treated as taxable
  • Interstate and export sales taxed in error
  • Use tax self-assessed on exempt purchases
  • Tax collected in error from customers and remitted to the state

The refund claim process

Refund claims must be filed within the statute of limitations — generally three years from the due date of the overpaid return. We identify eligible periods, prepare and file the claim, and represent you through any CDTFA review or examination of the refund.

California Excise Tax

The CDTFA administers California's excise taxes in addition to sales and use tax. Excise taxes on cannabis, fuel, alcohol, and tobacco products carry their own compliance requirements, reporting obligations, and audit procedures — and the consequences of non-compliance can be severe.

Excise tax areas we cover

  • Cannabis excise tax — cultivation tax and retail excise tax
  • Fuel taxes — motor vehicle fuel, diesel, jet fuel, and alternative fuels
  • Alcohol and spirits excise tax obligations
  • Cigarette and tobacco products tax
  • Electronic cigarette excise tax (ECET)

How we help

We advise on proper excise tax classification, registration, and reporting; represent businesses in CDTFA excise tax audits; assist with refund claims; and help businesses that have fallen behind come into compliance efficiently. Excise tax audits require specialized knowledge of CDTFA procedures that differ meaningfully from sales tax examinations.
