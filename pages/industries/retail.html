Retail: California's largest CDTFA audit segment

Retail businesses make up the largest share of CDTFA-registered taxpayers in California — and a corresponding share of the CDTFA's annual audit activity. Whether you operate a single storefront, a multi-location chain, or an e-commerce operation, the CDTFA has well-established methods for examining retail businesses and identifying discrepancies.

The good news is that most retail audit issues are identifiable in advance — and fixable before an auditor finds them.

What triggers a retail CDTFA audit

  • Low effective tax rate. The CDTFA tracks the ratio of taxable sales to total sales by industry. If your ratio falls significantly below the benchmark for similar retailers, you're a more likely audit candidate.
  • Bank deposit discrepancies. Auditors regularly reconcile reported gross sales against bank deposits. Unexplained gaps — deposits significantly exceeding reported sales — suggest unreported revenue.
  • Resale certificate issues. Invalid, expired, or improperly completed resale certificates result in exemptions being disallowed and tax assessments being issued.
  • Cash-intensive operations. High cash sales volume correlates with elevated audit selection rates. Auditors use markup analysis and unit-count methods to estimate sales at cash-heavy businesses.
  • Prior audit history. Businesses with prior CDTFA audit findings are at elevated risk of re-examination — particularly if the same issues haven't been corrected.

Taxability issues retailers commonly get wrong

  • Food and grocery items. If you sell any food products, the taxable vs. exempt distinction (hot/cold, on-premises/off-premises, carbonated/non-carbonated) is more complex than most retailers realize and easy to misapply at the POS level.
  • Digital goods and software. Software delivered electronically is generally not taxable as a sale of tangible personal property in California, but related services and subscriptions may have different treatment.
  • Bundled products. When taxable and exempt items are sold together at a single price, taxability of the bundle depends on its primary purpose and how it's described. Systematic errors here accumulate quickly.
  • Out-of-state sales. Sales shipped directly to out-of-state customers from California should generally not be subject to California sales tax — but the shipping documentation must support the exclusion.

Resale certificate management

For retailers who make wholesale or exempt sales, resale certificate management is one of the highest-risk compliance areas. Certificates must be timely, properly completed, from buyers whose permit is active, and consistent with the buyer's actual resale business. Invalid certificates result in the seller — not the buyer — owing the uncollected tax.

We help retailers implement certificate collection and verification processes that hold up during audits, and we defend exemption claims when auditors challenge certificates already on file.

E-commerce and nexus considerations

California-based retailers selling to out-of-state customers need to understand when those sales are excluded from California taxable sales and when they may create registration obligations in other states. Retailers who have been including out-of-state shipments in California taxable sales have overpaid — and may have a significant refund opportunity.

How we help retailers

  • Audit defense. We manage CDTFA retail audits end to end — reviewing your POS data, bank reconciliations, and resale certificates before the auditor does, and challenging unfavorable methodologies.
  • Taxability review. We review your product catalog and point-of-sale configuration to identify items that are being incorrectly taxed or incorrectly exempted.
  • Refund recovery. Many retailers have overpaid by including exempt sales or out-of-state shipments in taxable sales. We identify overpayments and file refund claims within the statute of limitations.
  • Compliance consulting. We help you build the record-keeping and reconciliation processes that reduce your audit risk going forward.
