California Cannabis Sales Tax Consulting

California cannabis businesses face excise tax, sales tax, METRC compliance, and aggressive CDTFA scrutiny. We help dispensaries and operators defend audits and stay compliant.

The most complex sales tax environment in California

California cannabis businesses face a tax structure unlike any other industry in the state — layered excise tax, state sales tax, local cannabis taxes, and the METRC track-and-trace system that gives the CDTFA unprecedented visibility into your sales data. Getting it right requires specialized knowledge. Getting it wrong invites audits that are detailed, data-driven, and expensive.

The California cannabis tax stack

  • Cannabis excise tax (15%). Collected at retail on gross receipts from cannabis sales. Administered by the CDTFA separately from sales tax — with its own filing requirements, reconciliation obligations, and audit procedures.
  • State sales tax (7.25%+). All retail cannabis sales are subject to California sales tax. Cannabis is not exempt as a food product.
  • Local cannabis taxes. Many California jurisdictions impose additional local taxes on cannabis businesses — calculated on gross receipts, weight, or units sold. Effective total tax burdens in high-tax cities can exceed 35–40% of retail price.

Why the CDTFA audits cannabis businesses aggressively

The CDTFA audits cannabis businesses at elevated rates because the industry is cash-heavy, newly regulated, and has a history of unlicensed operations. The METRC track-and-trace system gives auditors a powerful tool: every tagged cannabis package from cultivation through retail is recorded, and auditors routinely compare METRC data directly against your reported gross receipts. Discrepancies are an automatic audit trigger.

What CDTFA cannabis audits examine

  • METRC reconciliation. Auditors compare your track-and-trace records against reported sales. Waste, returns, promotional items, and theft all need to be documented and reconciled.
  • Excise tax collection and remittance. Every retail sale must have excise tax collected and remitted correctly. Rate errors, missing collections on specific product categories, and reconciliation failures are common findings.
  • POS system data. Your dispensary POS (BLAZE, Flowhub, Dutchie, etc.) is a primary audit resource. Auditors reconcile POS reports against METRC data and CDTFA filings.
  • Promotional items and samples. California generally treats promotional cannabis given to customers as a taxable retail sale at fair market value — meaning excise tax and sales tax may be owed on items given away for free.
  • Bank deposits. Even cash-heavy businesses maintain some banking relationship. Auditors reconcile deposits against reported gross receipts.

Common compliance failures we see

  • Excise tax not collected on all retail transactions
  • Excise tax calculation errors — wrong rate or incorrect gross receipt basis
  • METRC discrepancies without supporting documentation
  • Failure to file or remit on time due to cash flow pressure
  • Inconsistent treatment of returns, waste, and promotional items
  • Inadequate separation of excise tax and sales tax records

How we help cannabis businesses

  • Audit appeals. We represent cannabis businesses at the CDTFA Appeals Bureau and Office of Tax Appeals when audit determinations are incorrect or excessive.
